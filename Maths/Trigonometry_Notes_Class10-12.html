Class 10 — Introduction to Trigonometry

NCERT Ch. 8 & 9

Foundations: ratios in a right triangle, standard angle values, identities, and heights & distances.

01 Trigonometric Ratios

▾

In a right triangle with the right angle at B, and ∠A as the reference angle: the side opposite ∠A is the perpendicular, the side opposite the right angle is the hypotenuse, and the remaining side is the base.

sin A = Perpendicular / Hypotenuse   cos A = Base / Hypotenuse   tan A = Perpendicular / Base
cosec A = 1 / sin A   sec A = 1 / cos A   cot A = 1 / tan A
tan A = sin A / cos A   cot A = cos A / sin A

These six ratios depend only on the angle A, not on the size of the triangle (similar triangles give identical ratios).

03 Ratios of Complementary Angles

▾

Two angles are complementary if they add up to 90°.

sin(90° − A) = cos A   cos(90° − A) = sin A
tan(90° − A) = cot A   cot(90° − A) = tan A
sec(90° − A) = cosec A   cosec(90° − A) = sec A

04 Trigonometric Identities

▾
sin²A + cos²A = 1
1 + tan²A = sec²A (divide identity 1 by cos²A)
1 + cot²A = cosec²A (divide identity 1 by sin²A)

These identities are used to simplify expressions, eliminate the angle, or prove one trig expression equals another — a very common board question type.

05 Heights and Distances

▾

Angle of elevation: the angle between the horizontal line of sight and the line of sight to an object above it.

Angle of depression: the angle between the horizontal line and the line of sight to an object below it.

tan θ = Height / Distance (horizontal base)

Method: draw a clear diagram → mark the right angle → identify which sides are known/unknown relative to θ → pick the ratio (sin, cos or tan) connecting them → solve.

02 Sign of Functions in Each Quadrant (ASTC Rule)

▾

All (QI) → Sin, cosec (QII) → Tan, cot (QIII) → Cos, sec (QIV) are positive; every other ratio in that quadrant is negative. Mnemonic: "Add Sugar To Coffee".

QuadrantPositiveNegative

III (180°–270°)tan, cotsin, cos, sec, cosec
IV (270°–360°)cos, secsin, tan, cot, cosec

04 Trigonometric Functions of Allied Angles

▾

Allied angles are angles of the form −θ, 90°±θ, 180°±θ, 270°±θ, 360°±θ. Rule of thumb: for 90°±θ and 270°±θ, the function changes co-function (sin↔cos, tan↔cot, sec↔cosec); for 180°±θ and 360°±θ, the function stays the same. The sign is fixed by the quadrant of the angle.

sin(−θ) = −sin θ   cos(−θ) = cos θ   tan(−θ) = −tan θ
sin(180° − θ) = sin θ   cos(180° − θ) = −cos θ   tan(180° − θ) = −tan θ
sin(180° + θ) = −sin θ   cos(180° + θ) = −cos θ   tan(180° + θ) = tan θ
sin(90° − θ) = cos θ   cos(90° − θ) = sin θ   tan(90° − θ) = cot θ
sin(90° + θ) = cos θ   cos(90° + θ) = −sin θ   tan(90° + θ) = −cot θ

05 Compound Angle (Sum & Difference) Formulas

▾
sin(A + B) = sin A cos B + cos A sin B
sin(A − B) = sin A cos B − cos A sin B
cos(A + B) = cos A cos B − sin A sin B
cos(A − B) = cos A cos B + sin A sin B
tan(A + B) = (tan A + tan B) / (1 − tan A tan B)
tan(A − B) = (tan A − tan B) / (1 + tan A tan B)
cot(A + B) = (cot A cot B − 1) / (cot A + cot B)

06 Multiple & Sub-multiple Angle Formulas

▾
sin 2A = 2 sin A cos A = 2 tan A / (1 + tan²A)
cos 2A = cos²A − sin²A = 2cos²A − 1 = 1 − 2sin²A = (1 − tan²A)/(1 + tan²A)
tan 2A = 2 tan A / (1 − tan²A)
sin 3A = 3 sin A − 4 sin³A
cos 3A = 4 cos³A − 3 cos A
tan 3A = (3 tan A − tan³A) / (1 − 3 tan²A)

Same formulas hold for A/2 in place of A (sub-multiple / half-angle case) — e.g. sin A = 2 sin(A/2) cos(A/2).

07 Sum ⇄ Product Transformations

▾

Sum/Difference → Product

sin C + sin D = 2 sin((C+D)/2) cos((C−D)/2)
sin C − sin D = 2 cos((C+D)/2) sin((C−D)/2)
cos C + cos D = 2 cos((C+D)/2) cos((C−D)/2)
cos C − cos D = −2 sin((C+D)/2) sin((C−D)/2)

Product → Sum/Difference

2 sin A cos B = sin(A+B) + sin(A−B)
2 cos A sin B = sin(A+B) − sin(A−B)
2 cos A cos B = cos(A+B) + cos(A−B)
2 sin A sin B = cos(A−B) − cos(A+B)

08 Trigonometric Equations — General Solutions

▾
sin θ = sin α ⟹ θ = nπ + (−1)ⁿ α, n ∈ Z
cos θ = cos α ⟹ θ = 2nπ ± α, n ∈ Z
tan θ = tan α ⟹ θ = nπ + α, n ∈ Z
sin θ = 0 ⟹ θ = nπ   cos θ = 0 ⟹ θ = (2n+1)π/2

Class 12 — Inverse Trigonometric Functions

NCERT Ch. 2

Restricting domains to define inverses, their principal value branches, graphs, and the key identities used in simplification and equations.

01 Definition & Principal Value Branch

▾

Trigonometric functions aren't one-one over their full domain, so they aren't invertible everywhere. Restricting each to an interval where it is one-one and onto gives its inverse trigonometric function, written sin⁻¹x, cos⁻¹x, etc. (Note: sin⁻¹x ≠ 1/sin x.)

If sin y = x, then y = sin⁻¹x, provided −1 ≤ x ≤ 1 and y ∈ [−π/2, π/2]

The interval chosen for y is called the principal value branch.

▾

03 Graph Shapes (quick recall)

▾
  • sin⁻¹x: increasing, S-shaped curve through the origin, bounded between −π/2 and π/2.
  • cos⁻¹x: decreasing curve from (−1, π) to (1, 0), passing through (0, π/2).
  • tan⁻¹x: increasing S-curve with horizontal asymptotes at y = ±π/2, passes through origin.
  • cot⁻¹x: decreasing curve with asymptotes at y = 0 and y = π, passes through (0, π/2).
  • sec⁻¹x and cosec⁻¹x: defined only for |x| ≥ 1, with a gap in the middle of the range at π/2 (sec⁻¹) or 0 (cosec⁻¹).

04 Core Properties

▾

Set 1 — Basic (self-cancelling)

sin⁻¹(sin θ) = θ, θ ∈ [−π/2, π/2]   sin(sin⁻¹x) = x, x ∈ [−1, 1]

(similarly for cos, tan, cot, sec, cosec with their own principal domains)

Set 2 — Reciprocal

sin⁻¹(1/x) = cosec⁻¹x   cos⁻¹(1/x) = sec⁻¹x   tan⁻¹(1/x) = cot⁻¹x (for x > 0)

Set 3 — Complementary (co-function sum = π/2)

sin⁻¹x + cos⁻¹x = π/2   tan⁻¹x + cot⁻¹x = π/2   sec⁻¹x + cosec⁻¹x = π/2

Set 4 — Negative argument

sin⁻¹(−x) = −sin⁻¹x   tan⁻¹(−x) = −tan⁻¹x   cosec⁻¹(−x) = −cosec⁻¹x
cos⁻¹(−x) = π − cos⁻¹x   cot⁻¹(−x) = π − cot⁻¹x   sec⁻¹(−x) = π − sec⁻¹x

05 Sum & Difference Formulas

▾
tan⁻¹x + tan⁻¹y = tan⁻¹((x+y)/(1−xy))  valid when xy < 1
tan⁻¹x − tan⁻¹y = tan⁻¹((x−y)/(1+xy))  valid when xy > −1
sin⁻¹x + sin⁻¹y = sin⁻¹(x√(1−y²) + y√(1−x²))  valid when x²+y² ≤ 1
cos⁻¹x + cos⁻¹y = cos⁻¹(xy − √(1−x²)√(1−y²))

When xy > 1 in the tan⁻¹ sum formula, add π (for x, y > 0) or subtract π (for x, y < 0) to the RHS.

06 Double Angle Formulas (Inverse Form)

▾
2 tan⁻¹x = sin⁻¹(2x / (1+x²)), |x| ≤ 1
2 tan⁻¹x = cos⁻¹((1−x²)/(1+x²)), x ≥ 0
2 tan⁻¹x = tan⁻¹(2x / (1−x²)), −1 < x < 1
